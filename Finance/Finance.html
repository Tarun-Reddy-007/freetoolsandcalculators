Frequently Asked Questions

Which calculator should I use for my loan?

Use Loan EMI for general loans, Car Loan EMI for vehicles, Personal Loan EMI for unsecured loans, Home Loan Eligibility for mortgage planning, and Loan Affordability to understand maximum safe borrowing. Each addresses different borrowing scenarios.

How accurate are these calculations?

These calculators use standard financial formulas (EMI formula, compound interest formula, DTI calculations) that match real lender calculations. Results are accurate for single-country scenarios. For complex situations, verify with your lender.

Should I compare multiple lender offers?

Absolutely. Use these calculators to test each lender's offer (with their rate and fees) using same principal and tenure. Compare EMIs and total interest to find the best deal. Even 0.5% rate difference impacts thousands of rupees.

How do I optimize my loan terms?

Test scenarios: 1) Larger down payment 2) Shorter tenure 3) Higher extra payments 4) Lower interest rate. Use these calculators to model each and find the optimal balance between monthly affordability and total interest cost.

What's the impact of extra payments?

Massive. Even small extra payments ($50-100/month) reduce total interest by 15-25% and shorten tenure by years. Use the extra payment feature in loan calculators to see exact savings for your scenario.

Should I consider refinancing my existing loan?

Yes if rates drop significantly or credit score improves. Use these calculators to compare: remaining balance × new rate vs original loan interest. If new loan savings exceed refinancing fees, refinance.

How does compound interest help long-term investing?

Dramatically. Using the compound interest calculator, you'll see small monthly contributions compound to hundreds of thousands over decades. The earlier you start, the more time interest has to grow exponentially.

What's my realistic borrowing capacity?

Use Loan Affordability Calculator with conservative DTI (36-40% instead of max 43-50%) and safety buffer (-5% to -10%). This shows comfortable borrowing that maintains financial flexibility for emergencies.

Do these calculators account for taxes and insurance?

These calculators focus on interest and principal calculations. Taxes, insurance, registration, HOA fees, and other costs should be added separately for complete financial planning. Use results as foundation for broader budgeting.

Is my financial data completely private?

100% private. All calculations happen entirely in your browser. No loan amounts, rates, EMIs, or any financial data is sent to servers, stored, or tracked. Your financial privacy is completely protected at all times.
