Car Loan EMI Calculator

About EMI

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is the fixed payment amount you make every month toward a loan (principal + interest).

  • Formula: EMI = P r x (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1)
  • P = Principal, r = Monthly Interest Rate, n = Number of Months

Car Loan EMI Calculator FAQ

How is car loan EMI calculated?

EMI uses the formula P*r*(1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1) where P is principal, r monthly interest (annual rate / 12 / 100) and n number of months.

What factors affect my EMI amount?

Principal financed (after down payment), interest rate, tenure length, and any processing fees or extra payments influence EMI or total cost.

What is the difference between flat and reducing balance interest?

Reducing balance interest is charged on outstanding principal each month (standard for most bank auto loans). Flat rate computes interest on the original principal for the full tenure, making effective cost higher.

How do monthly extra payments help?

Extra payments reduce principal faster, shortening the loan duration and lowering total interest. The calculator estimates months and interest saved.

What is a processing fee?

A processing (origination) fee is a lender charge, often a small percent of principal. It increases upfront or total cost and should be included when comparing offers.
