About EMI

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is the fixed payment amount you make every month toward a loan (principal + interest).

  • Formula: EMI = P r x (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1)
  • P = Principal, r = Monthly Interest Rate, n = Number of Months

Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is EMI and how does it work?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount you pay every month toward your car loan. Each payment includes both principal (the amount borrowed) and interest. Early payments are mostly interest; later payments mostly principal. The calculator shows your exact EMI amount.

What does the processing fee do?

Processing fees (typically 0.5-2% of loan amount) are charges lenders add for loan origination. They can be paid upfront or financed into the loan. This calculator shows how fees impact your total interest cost either way.

How does making extra payments help?

Extra monthly payments reduce your principal faster, which means less interest accumulates. Even small extra payments ($25-100/month) can save thousands in total interest and shorten your loan tenure by months or years.

What's a reasonable down payment for a car?

Most lenders recommend 10-20% down to avoid underwater loans (owing more than car value). Larger down payments reduce your EMI, total interest, and risk. Use this calculator to see how different down payments affect your monthly payment.

Should I choose a shorter or longer loan tenure?

Shorter tenures (3-5 years) mean higher EMI but much lower total interest. Longer tenures (6-8 years) mean lower EMI but significantly higher total interest. Balance your monthly affordability against total cost using this calculator.

What interest rate should I expect?

Car loan rates depend on credit score, down payment, tenure, vehicle age, and market conditions. Current rates typically range from 3-10%. Check with your bank or credit union for pre-approval rates before shopping for cars.

Can I prepay my car loan early without penalties?

Most car loans in the US allow prepayment without penalties. However, check your loan agreement to confirm. Some older loans have prepayment penalties, though these are increasingly rare in modern auto financing.

How do I compare loan offers from different lenders?

Get the Effective APR (Annual Percentage Rate) from each lender, which includes all fees. Then use this calculator with the same principal, rate, and tenure for each offer. Compare total interest costs and EMI amounts to find the best deal.

What's the difference between simple and reducing balance interest?

Reducing balance (used in most car loans) calculates interest on outstanding balance each month. Simple interest calculates on original principal. Reducing balance is cheaper overall and more common in auto financing.
