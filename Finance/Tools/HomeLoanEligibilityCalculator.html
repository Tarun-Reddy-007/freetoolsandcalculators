Home Loan Eligibility Calculator

How Eligibility is Estimated

Lenders approximate maximum home loan by looking at your gross monthly income, existing debt obligations, target back-end DTI (Debt-to-Income) ratio, interest rate and tenure.

  • Income: Gross monthly pre-tax.
  • Existing Debt: Monthly payments (loans, credit cards).
  • DTI: (Existing Debt + New EMI) / Gross Income.
  • Rule: Target DTI ratio must not exceed chosen threshold.
  • EMI Formula: P = EMI * ((1+r)^n -1) / (r (1+r)^n)

Home Loan Eligibility FAQ

How is home loan eligibility calculated?

Eligibility is based on gross monthly income, existing debt obligations, target debt-to-income (DTI) threshold, interest rate and loan tenure. Maximum EMI is derived from allowed DTI then converted to a principal amount using the EMI formula.

What is Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio?

DTI = (Existing monthly debt payments + Proposed housing EMI) / Gross monthly income. Lenders use back-end DTI to gauge repayment capacity.

Which DTI ratio do lenders prefer?

Common back-end DTI caps are near 36%, 43% (typical), and sometimes up to 50% for certain programs. Lower ratios improve approval odds and terms.

How can I increase my home loan eligibility?

Raise income, reduce existing debts, improve credit score, increase down payment, or select a longer tenure to lower EMI and DTI.

Does down payment affect eligibility?

Yes. A larger down payment reduces the financed amount, improves loan-to-value, may lower EMI, and can strengthen approval chances or rate offers.
