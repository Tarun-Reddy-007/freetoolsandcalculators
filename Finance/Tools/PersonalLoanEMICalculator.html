Frequently Asked Questions

What is a personal loan?

An unsecured loan with no collateral, offering quick funding at fixed interest rates. Used for emergencies, education, weddings, home improvement, debt consolidation, and business needs. Higher rates than secured loans but faster approval.

Why are personal loan rates higher than other loans?

No collateral means higher risk for lenders, so they charge higher rates. Personal loans typically range 10-18% depending on credit score and lender. Better credit scores get lower rates. This calculator shows impact of different rates on your EMI.

How does tenure affect personal loan EMI?

Longer tenure = lower monthly EMI but higher total interest. For example, $100,000 at 15% for 24 months vs 60 months: lower EMI in 60-month, but pay $30,000+ more interest. Test different tenures with this calculator to find your balance.

What's a reasonable personal loan amount?

Most banks lend based on income and credit score, typically 10-25 times monthly salary up to a maximum. Common amounts: $5,000 - $1,000,000. Use this calculator to see what you can comfortably afford before applying.

What's the processing fee for personal loans?

Typically 1-3% of loan amount. Can be paid upfront or financed into the loan. This calculator shows impact both ways. Paying upfront avoids interest on the fee itself, often saving money overall. Compare both options.

Are there prepayment penalties for personal loans?

Most modern personal loans have no prepayment penalties. However, check your loan agreement to confirm. This calculator shows massive savings possible through prepayment, so look for loans allowing penalty-free extra payments.

How much can I save with extra monthly prepayments?

Enormous savings are possible. Extra $200/month can save 20-30% of total interest and shorten tenure by 3-5 years. Use this calculator to see exact savings for your scenario. Even small extra payments compound meaningfully.

Should I use personal loan for high-interest debt consolidation?

Yes, if personal loan rate is lower than credit card rates (usually is). Consolidate multiple debts into one lower-rate loan. However, don't use freed credit cards for new spending. Calculate exact savings using this tool before consolidating.

What credit score do I need for personal loans?

Minimum 600-650 typically. Higher scores (750+) get better rates. Calculate EMI differences using this tool with typical rates for your credit range to understand potential offers before applying.

Is my personal financial data secure?

Completely secure. All calculations happen entirely in your browser. No loan amounts, rates, or EMI information is ever sent to servers or stored. Your financial privacy is fully protected at all times.
