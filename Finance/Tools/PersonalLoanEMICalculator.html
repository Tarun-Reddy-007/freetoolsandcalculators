Personal Loan EMI FAQ

How is personal loan EMI calculated?

Using EMI = P * r * (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1) where P principal, r monthly interest (annual / 12 / 100), n total months in tenure.

What interest rates are typical for personal loans?

Unsecured personal loans often carry higher rates than secured loans; ranges vary by market and credit score. Compare APR including fees.

How can I reduce my EMI burden?

Choose a longer tenure, negotiate a lower rate, improve credit score, or make principal part prepayments to cut remaining EMI duration.

Do extra monthly prepayments always save interest?

Yes, if applied to principal and no penalty applies. They shorten tenure and reduce total interest. Confirm lender prepayment policy first.

What is a processing fee?

A one-time lender charge (percent of principal) added upfront. It raises effective APR but is not part of principal for EMI math.
