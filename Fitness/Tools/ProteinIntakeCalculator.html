Protein Intake Calculator

Why Protein?

Protein supports muscle repair, hormonal signaling, immune function, satiety and metabolic health. Needs scale with lean mass, training stress, energy deficit and age.

General Evidence-Based Ranges

Minimal: 0.8 g/kg (RDA – prevents deficiency).
General Health: 1.0–1.4 g/kg.
Muscle Gain / Active: 1.4–1.8 g/kg.
Fat Loss (lean preservation): 1.6–2.2 g/kg.
High Demand / Athletes: up to ~2.4 g/kg (sometimes transiently).

Individualization matters: consult a professional for medical or clinical conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much protein do I need per day?

It depends on your goal and activity level. Sedentary adults need 0.8 g/kg, moderately active need 1.2-1.6 g/kg, and muscle builders need 1.6-2.2 g/kg. Use this calculator to get your personalized range based on your inputs.

Does body weight matter more than activity level?

Both matter. Heavier individuals need more total protein, but per-kilogram requirements increase with training intensity. This calculator factors both into its recommendations.

Can I eat all my protein in one meal?

While possible, spreading protein across meals (20-40g per meal) optimizes muscle protein synthesis. Your body can't use excessive protein in a single meal—the rest becomes calories. Distribute intake for best results.

What's the difference between plant and animal protein?

Animal proteins are complete (all amino acids); most plant proteins are incomplete. Combine different plant sources or add animal protein to ensure all essential amino acids. Use this calculator for total protein; track amino acid completeness separately.

Does protein intake affect kidney health?

For healthy individuals, even 2.0-2.2 g/kg is safe. People with kidney disease should consult doctors about protein limits. Research shows no kidney damage from high protein in healthy people.

Should I adjust protein when cutting or bulking?

Increase protein when cutting to preserve muscle during calorie deficit. When bulking, maintain higher protein (1.6-2.0 g/kg) to maximize muscle gain relative to fat gain. Use this calculator to adjust for your phase.

How does age affect protein requirements?

Older adults need slightly higher protein (1.0-1.2 g/kg) to combat age-related muscle loss. Athletes of any age maintain elevated requirements. Use the activity level setting to account for training intensity.

Is excess protein stored as fat?

Excess protein above your calorie needs can be converted to fat, just like carbs or fat. However, protein has the highest thermic effect (20-30% of calories burned during digestion), making it harder to over-consume.

Should I take protein supplements?

Supplements are convenient but not necessary. Whole foods should be your primary source. If you can't meet targets through food (due to budget, preference, or schedule), supplements fill the gap affordably.

How often should I recalculate as I progress?

Recalculate when body weight changes significantly (5+ lbs), training intensity shifts notably, or your goal changes. Even small changes in weight or activity affect recommendations. Check quarterly during active training phases.
