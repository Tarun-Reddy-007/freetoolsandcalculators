Health Calculators FAQ

What is the difference between BMI and Body Fat %?

BMI uses height and weight only as a population screening, while body fat % estimates actual adipose proportion for a more individualized insight.

How is BMR different from daily calorie needs?

BMR reflects resting energy; daily needs add activity and lifestyle multipliers to estimate total maintenance calories.

How much water should I drink?

Intake varies by weight, activity, climate; the hydration calculator provides a baseline—adjust for medical advice and individual cues.

Are heart rate zones the same for everyone?

Zones are estimates using age or max HR formulas; training history and genetics cause individual variation—field tests refine accuracy.

How should I use the sleep cycle calculator?

Plan wake or bed times around ~90‑minute cycles to reduce grogginess; prioritize total sleep quality and consistency over exact cycle counts.
