BMI Calculator

What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) estimates body fat using height & weight. It helps classify: underweight, normal, overweight or obese.

  • Under < 18.5
  • Normal 18.5-24.9
  • Over 25-29.9
  • Obese ≥ 30

BMI is a screening metric; consult a professional for full assessment.

About BMI Calculator

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple yet effective screening tool used globally to assess whether a person's weight is healthy relative to their height. While BMI doesn't directly measure body fat or muscle mass, it provides a quick baseline for identifying potential health risks associated with being underweight, overweight, or obese.

Our BMI Calculator supports metric and imperial units, instantly classifies results into WHO-standard categories, and offers actionable insights. Whether beginning a health journey or monitoring progress, understanding BMI is a valuable first step toward informed decisions about nutrition, activity, and preventive care.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does BMI actually measure?

BMI is weight (kg) divided by height (m) squared. It estimates body fatness using population-level screening but does not directly measure fat or muscle.

Why do athletes often have high BMI?

Muscle is denser than fat. Trained athletes can have overweight BMI despite low body fat. Body composition analysis is more informative for athletes.

Is BMI the same for men and women?

Yes. BMI uses same cutoffs. Women typically have higher body fat at the same BMI due to physiology, but standard WHO categories apply equally.

What is a healthy BMI range?

WHO: Underweight <18.5, Normal 18.5-24.9, Overweight 25-29.9, Obese ≥30. The 18.5-24.9 range generally represents lower health risk.

How often should I check my BMI?

Monthly or after lifestyle changes is reasonable. Daily checks amplify normal fluctuations. Track trends over 4-8 weeks for meaningful insight.

Can BMI misclassify someone as unhealthy?

Yes. High BMI can reflect muscle, bone, or frame size. Normal BMI doesn't guarantee health without cardiovascular fitness and favorable metabolic markers. Use with waist circumference.

How does height affect BMI accuracy?

BMI scales with height squared, so very tall/short individuals may see distortions. Waist-to-height ratio or direct body composition improves accuracy for extreme heights.

Should I aim for the lowest possible BMI?

No. Extremely low BMI can reflect poor nutrition or muscle loss. A sustainable BMI that maintains energy and strength is healthier than chasing the lowest number.

Does BMI change with age?

BMI calculation is age-independent. However, body composition shifts with age. Muscle declines naturally, so resistance training becomes more important over time.

What's the best next step after calculating BMI?

Measure waist circumference, check body fat percentage, review metabolic markers (BP, glucose, lipids), and assess fitness. Combine these with professional guidance for holistic health picture.
