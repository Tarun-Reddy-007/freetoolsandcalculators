Heart Rate Zone Calculator

About Zones

Heart rate training zones are intensity ranges based on estimated maximum heart rate (HRmax) or heart rate reserve (HRR). They help structure endurance, fat oxidation, and performance training.

Common Formulas

220 - Age: Simple HRmax estimate.
Tanaka: 208 - 0.7 × Age (often closer for adults).
Karvonen (HRR): Uses (HRmax - Resting HR) × % + Resting HR.

Estimates only; individual lab or field testing is more accurate.

Heart Rate Zone Calculator FAQ

How are heart rate zones determined?

Zones are calculated as percentages of HRmax or heart rate reserve (HRR) when resting heart rate is provided (Karvonen method).

What formula is most accurate for HRmax?

Tanaka (208 - 0.7 × Age) often improves over 220 - Age; lab tests or field max efforts are more precise.

Why include resting heart rate?

Resting HR personalizes zones using HRR, reflecting fitness adaptations better than %HRmax alone.

How can I train in Zone 2 effectively?

Maintain conversational pace near 60–70% HRmax or 60–70% HRR for aerobic base and fat oxidation gains.

How often should zones be recalculated?

Every 6–8 weeks or after noticeable fitness improvements or testing changes; update resting HR when it declines.
