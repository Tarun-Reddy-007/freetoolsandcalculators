What Is 'Ideal Weight'?

There is no single perfect number. This tool gives estimates from common formulas plus a BMI-based healthy range to inform—not prescribe—goals.

Formulas Included

Devine (1974), Robinson (1983), Miller (1983), Hamwi (1964) — all linear height models originally for medication dosing or population norms.

Ideal Weight Calculator FAQ

How do the ideal weight formulas differ?

Devine, Robinson, Miller and Hamwi use linear height adjustments with sex-specific bases; differences arise from original clinical contexts and data sets.

Are ideal weight formulas outdated?

They are still referenced but were not designed for individualized physique goals; body composition and health markers matter more today.

What factors influence a healthy weight besides height?

Muscle mass, bone structure, genetics, age, ethnicity, hormones, lifestyle and medical conditions all affect a healthy target weight.

Should I focus on ideal weight or body composition?

Prioritize sustainable habits and body composition (fat vs lean mass). Formula weights are reference points, not absolute goals.

How often should I recalculate ideal weight?

Height in adults is stable; revisit estimates only when refining goals or after major composition changes (muscle gain or loss).

What is a safe rate of weight change?

Common guidance: loss ~0.5–1% body weight per week; gain (lean focus) ~0.25–0.5% per week depending on training status.
