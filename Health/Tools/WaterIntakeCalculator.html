Daily Water Intake Calculator

Why Water Intake Matters

This tool estimates an individualized daily water goal using body weight, activity, climate, pregnancy/nursing, and caffeine modifiers.

Base Formula

Base need ≈ 35 ml per kg of body weight (general guideline, mid‑range of 30–40 ml).

Adjustments

  • + 350 ml per 30 min of exercise (sweat)
  • + 5-10% for hot / very dry climate
  • + 300 ml if pregnant; + 700 ml if nursing
  • - ~150 ml per 200 mg caffeine (optional offset)

Estimates only. Medical conditions (kidney, heart, endocrine) may alter needs—consult a professional.

Daily Water Intake Calculator FAQ

How much water should I drink daily?

Needs vary; a mid guideline is ~35 ml per kg. The calculator adjusts for exercise, climate, pregnancy/nursing & caffeine.

Does coffee count toward hydration?

Yes, most beverages contribute. High caffeine has a mild diuretic effect; an optional offset is applied here per 200 mg.

Should I adjust water intake for exercise?

Yes. Approx +350 ml per 30 minutes of sweat‑producing activity helps replace fluid losses.

What are signs of dehydration?

Dark urine, dry mouth, headache, fatigue, dizziness, infrequent urination. Persistent symptoms warrant medical advice.

Can you drink too much water?

Yes. Excessive rapid intake can dilute sodium (hyponatremia). Spread intake; include electrolytes if volumes are high.

How should I pace water intake?

Distribute across the day: morning priming, steady daytime sips, extra around exercise, taper slightly before bed.
