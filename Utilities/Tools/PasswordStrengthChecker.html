  • Detects use of numbers, symbols, and mixed case

Password Strength Checker FAQ

How is password strength calculated?

The checker scores length, mixed case, numbers, symbols and flags repeated or common patterns to produce a strength rating.

What makes a strong password?

High entropy: 12+ characters with random mix of upper, lower, numbers and symbols; avoiding dictionary words, sequences and repeats.

Are my passwords sent to a server?

No. All evaluation runs locally in your browser; nothing is transmitted or stored.

Should I use a password manager?

Yes—managers generate and store unique complex passwords, reducing reuse and improving overall security hygiene.

Why avoid common patterns?

Attackers test predictable strings (e.g., "password123", keyboard sequences) first, drastically lowering cracking time if used.
