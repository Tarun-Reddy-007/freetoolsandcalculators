Roman Numeral Converter FAQ

How do I convert a number to Roman numerals?

Enter an integer (1–3999) and the tool maps descending values (M, CM, D, CD, etc.) appending symbols until the number is reduced to zero.

What range of numbers is supported?

Standard Roman numerals cover 1 through 3999. Larger values historically use overlines, which this tool does not implement.

How does subtractive notation work?

A smaller symbol before a larger one subtracts its value (IV = 4, IX = 9, XL = 40, CM = 900) to minimize repetition.

Why was my Roman numeral rejected?

It may use invalid repetition (e.g., VVV), incorrect subtractive pairs (IL), or represent a value outside 1–3999.

Can Roman numerals represent zero or negatives?

No. Classical Roman numerals have no symbol for zero and do not denote negative numbers.
