Unit Converter FAQ

How accurate are the conversions?

They use standard conversion factors; floating precision is rounded for readability. Critical engineering uses should verify independently.

Why do temperature conversions differ from others?

Temperature scales involve offsets (e.g., °F to °C subtract 32 before scaling) unlike multiplicative-only conversions for length or mass.

Can I convert negative temperatures?

Yes, negative Celsius or Fahrenheit values are valid and converted with the same formulas.

Why is the result rounded?

To prevent long repeating decimals. Internal precision remains high; display is trimmed for clarity.

Are conversions done online?

No—everything runs locally in your browser, preserving privacy and speed.
